Our Programs

Intro Seminars

What are they?

Our intro seminars are weekly sessions designed to make AI safety accessible to everyone. We explore fundamental concepts like alignment problems, capability control, and AI governance through interactive discussions, case studies, and practical examples.

No technical background is required - these sessions are perfect for students from any discipline who want to understand the challenges and opportunities in AI safety.

What to expect:

  • Interactive presentations on core AI safety topics
  • Group discussions and Q&A sessions
  • Real-world case studies and examples
  • Networking with like-minded students

In-Depth Reading Groups

Who should join?

  • Students with some technical background in CS, maths, or related fields
  • Those interested in the technical details of AI safety research
  • Anyone wanting to engage with cutting-edge research

Deep Dive into Research

Our reading groups provide an opportunity to engage deeply with the latest AI safety research. Led by PhD students and faculty members, these sessions focus on understanding technical papers, discussing methodologies, and exploring the implications of recent findings.

We cover both technical alignment research and AI governance/policy papers, ensuring a well-rounded understanding of the field.

Research Projects

Contribute to the Future

Our research program connects motivated students with faculty supervisors to work on cutting-edge AI safety problems. Whether you're an undergraduate looking for your first research experience or a graduate student seeking to specialize in AI safety, we provide structured opportunities to make meaningful contributions.

Projects range from theoretical work on alignment algorithms to empirical studies on AI behavior, and policy research on governance frameworks.

Opportunities include:

  • Independent research projects with faculty mentorship
  • Collaborative team projects with other students
  • Opportunities to present at conferences
  • Publication in peer-reviewed venues
Technical Research

Developing new algorithms and methods for AI safety

Empirical Studies

Testing and evaluating AI systems for safety properties

Policy Research

Analyzing governance frameworks and regulatory approaches

Success Stories

Our members have published papers at top-tier conferences including NeurIPS, ICML, and FAccT. Several students have gone on to PhD programs in AI safety, internships at leading AI labs, and positions in AI governance organizations. Our collaborative environment ensures that every participant gains valuable research experience and contributes to the global effort to ensure AI safety.
